### Item 3.1 — Map legend

Cut 08 had a color-key legend below the map:

```
[ primary ] original track   [ alert ] contested gap
[ secondary ] emergent identity   [ rule ] rule-applied recommendation
```

Operators currently guess what colors mean. Add a small legend overlay
at the bottom-right or top-left of the stage panel (NOT on the map
itself — sits in the panel chrome). Same operator-grade dark-mode
register as MapLayers.

Four entries:
- **Original track** — `--color-decision` swatch
- **Contested gap** — `--color-contested` swatch
- **Emergent identity** — `--color-resolved` swatch (new MMSI shows up)
- **Rule-applied recommendation** — `--color-decision` with dashed
  border (signals the prior-rule chip)

**File:** create `app/src/components/MapLegend.tsx` and mount in
`StageViewport.tsx` as a sibling to `MapLayers` and `DataSourcesChips`.

### Item 3.2 — Stage header (hero title/subtitle)

Cut 08 had a narrative header at the top of the stage panel before
the map: an eyebrow ("Substrate / Stage / Working / Command"), a
title (case-driven; e.g., "Identity churn at Strait of Hormuz"), and
a subtitle (e.g., "Vessel goes dark for 38 minutes; second identity
appears 4.2nm away"). Our app jumps straight from the panel header
("Stage / map · replay") into the map.

Add a stage-header band BELOW the panel header but ABOVE the map.
Three lines:
- **Eyebrow** — small uppercase mono, e.g., "PHASE 2 · DARK GAP"
- **Title** — Fraunces serif, ~16px, derived from selected case OR
  scenario state (default: "Watchfloor at rest" when no case selected)
- **Subtitle** — Fraunces serif italic, ~12px, derived from
  selected case OR a scenario-default subtitle

Driven by `scenarioState.phase` and `selectedAlert`. When no alert is
selected, falls back to scenario-state-derived defaults from the
SpeedRun cut script.

**File:** create `app/src/components/StageHeader.tsx` and mount in
`StageViewport.tsx` between the panel-header and the map.
